## üéØ The 4 Pillars of Effective Prompts

### 1. üé≠ **Role Definition**
```text
You are a [specific role] with [X years] of experience in [domain].
```

**Examples:**
- "You are a data analyst with 5 years of experience in e-commerce"
- "You are a marketing manager specializing in B2B SaaS"
- "You are a financial advisor helping young professionals"

### 2. üéØ **Clear Task**
```text
Your task is to [specific action] by [method/approach].
```

**Examples:**
- "Analyze this sales data and identify the top 3 trends"
- "Create a marketing strategy for our new product launch"
- "Write a summary report for the executive team"

### 3. üß† **Context Setting**
```text
Context: [background information, constraints, audience]
```

**Examples:**
- "Context: This is for a startup with 50 employees, limited budget"
- "Context: Target audience is C-level executives, 5-minute presentation"
- "Context: We're in the healthcare industry, compliance is critical"

### 4. üì¶ **Output Format**
```text
Format your response as: [specific structure]
```

**Examples:**
- "Format as: Executive summary + 3 key findings + recommendations"
- "Format as: Markdown table with columns for [data]"
- "Format as: Bullet points with supporting evidence"

## üé≠ Role Templates

### **Data Analyst**
```text
You are a senior data analyst with [X] years of experience in [industry]. You specialize in [specific expertise] and have a track record of [achievements]. You approach problems with [methodology] and communicate in a [style] manner.
```

### **Business Consultant**
```text
You are a management consultant with [X] years of experience at [firm type]. You specialize in [domain] and have helped [types of companies] solve [types of problems]. You approach challenges with [methodology] and prioritize [values].
```

### **Marketing Manager**
```text
You are a marketing manager with [X] years of experience in [industry]. You specialize in [marketing areas] and have a track record of [achievements]. You communicate with [audience] and focus on [goals].
```

### **CEO/Executive**
```text
You are a [C-level position] with [X] years of experience leading [company types]. You're responsible for [key areas] and have a track record of [achievements]. You think strategically about [focus areas] and prioritize [values].
```

## üìä Data Analysis Prompts

### **Basic Analysis**
```text
You are a data analyst. Analyze this data and provide:
1. Key metrics and statistics
2. Trends and patterns
3. Insights and implications
4. Recommendations and next steps

Data: [Your data here]
Format: Executive summary + detailed analysis
```

### **Customer Segmentation**
```text
You are a customer analytics expert. Segment this customer data and provide:
1. Natural customer groups based on behavior
2. Characteristics of each segment
3. Value and potential of each segment
4. Targeting strategies for each segment

Data: [Your customer data here]
Format: Segmentation report with tables and insights
```

### **Performance Review**
```text
You are a performance analyst. Review this performance data and provide:
1. Key performance indicators and trends
2. Period-over-period comparisons
3. Strengths and areas for improvement
4. Strategic recommendations

Data: [Your performance data here]
Format: Performance report with executive summary
```

## üîó Prompt Chaining Examples

### **Analysis ‚Üí Strategy ‚Üí Implementation**
```text
Step 1: "Analyze this data and identify the top 3 trends"
Step 2: "Based on these trends, develop a strategic plan"
Step 3: "Create an implementation roadmap for this plan"
Step 4: "Define success metrics and monitoring approach"
```

### **Problem ‚Üí Solution ‚Üí Validation**
```text
Step 1: "Identify and define the core problem"
Step 2: "Generate 3 potential solutions"
Step 3: "Evaluate and select the best solution"
Step 4: "Create a validation and testing plan"
```

## ‚ö° Quick Tips

### **For Better Results**
- Be specific about what you want
- Provide relevant context and background
- Use clear, actionable language
- Specify the exact output format
- Test and iterate on your prompts

### **Common Mistakes to Avoid**
- Being too vague ("help me with data")
- Not providing enough context
- Forgetting to specify output format
- Using overly complex prompts
- Not testing and iterating

### **When to Use Different Techniques**
- **Role-based prompting:** When you need specific expertise
- **Prompt chaining:** For complex, multi-step tasks
- **Few-shot learning:** When you have good examples
- **System prompts:** For consistent behavior across conversations
