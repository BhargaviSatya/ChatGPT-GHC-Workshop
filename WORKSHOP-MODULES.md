## ðŸŽ¯ Module 1: Introduction to Prompt Engineering (10 min)

### **Live Demo (3 minutes)**
**Show the difference between basic and engineered prompts:**

**Basic Prompt:**
```text
Explain inflation.
```

**Engineered Prompt:**
```text
You are a financial advisor. Explain inflation to a 25-year-old who just started their first job. Focus on:
1. What inflation means for their daily life
2. How it affects their savings
3. One practical tip to protect their money

Keep it under 200 words and use simple language.
```

**Ask the audience:** "What's the difference? Which one would you rather receive?"

### **Hands-on Exercise (5 minutes)**
**Task:** Create your first structured prompt

**Choose your scenario:**
- **Option A:** You're a fitness trainer helping someone get in shape
- **Option B:** You're a travel agent planning a vacation
- **Option C:** You're a career coach helping with job search

**Template:**
```text
You are a [ROLE]. 
Your task: [WHAT TO DO]
Context: [BACKGROUND INFO]
Format: [HOW TO PRESENT RESULTS]
```

**Example to try:**
```text
You are a study assistant.
Your task: Help a student understand complex topics
Context: Student preparing for Psychology final exam
Format: 1) Simple definition, 2) Real example, 3) Key points, 4) Practice question
Topic: Classical Conditioning
```

### **Group Share (2 minutes)**
- One person from each table shares their prompt
- **Vote:** Which table created the most effective prompt?
- Discuss what made it effective

## ðŸŽ­ Module 3: Role-Based Prompting (10 min)

### **Live Demo (3 minutes)**
**Show how roles change responses:**

**Same task, different roles:**
```text
Task: "Our customer retention dropped from 85% to 72%"

Role 1: "You are a data analyst..."
Role 2: "You are a CEO..."  
Role 3: "You are a customer success manager..."
```

**Ask:** "How would each role approach this differently?"

### **Hands-on Exercise (5 minutes)**
**Task:** Each table picks a role and tests it

**Roles to assign:**
- Table 1: Data Analyst (focus on metrics and trends)
- Table 2: Marketing Manager (focus on campaigns and messaging)
- Table 3: CEO (focus on strategy and business impact)
- Table 4: Customer Success Manager (focus on user experience)

**Test with this scenario:**
"Our website traffic increased 40% but conversions dropped 15%"

**Each table should:**
1. Create a role-specific prompt
2. Test it with ChatGPT
3. Prepare a 30-second summary of their role's perspective

### **Group Demo (2 minutes)**
- Each table demonstrates their role's perspective
- Discuss how roles change the approach

## ðŸ“Š Module 4: Data Analysis with ChatGPT (15 min)

### **Live Demo (5 minutes)**
**Complete data analysis example:**

```text
You are a senior data analyst with expertise in e-commerce analytics.

Analyze this customer data and provide:
1. Key metrics and trends
2. Customer segmentation
3. Business insights
4. Strategic recommendations

Data: [Show complex dataset on screen]

Format as: Executive summary + detailed analysis + action plan
```

### **Hands-on Exercise (8 minutes)**
**Task:** Each table analyzes the provided dataset

**Your dataset:**
```
Month | Revenue | Customers | Churn Rate | Support Tickets
Jan   | $50K    | 500      | 5%         | 25
Feb   | $55K    | 520      | 4%         | 30
Mar   | $52K    | 510      | 6%         | 28
Apr   | $60K    | 540      | 3%         | 22
```

**Your prompt should include:**
- Role: Data analyst with specific expertise
- Task: Analyze this data and provide insights
- Context: This is a SaaS company's Q1 performance
- Format: Executive summary + detailed findings + recommendations

**Deliverables:**
- 3 key findings
- 1 business recommendation
- 1 question for further analysis

### **Presentations (2 minutes)**
- Each table presents their insights (30 seconds each)
- **Award:** "Most Creative Insight" and "Most Actionable Recommendation"
- Discuss different approaches and findings

## âš¡ Module 5: Advanced Techniques (10 min)

### **Live Demo (3 minutes)**
**Show prompt chaining:**

```text
Step 1: "Analyze this data and identify the top 3 trends"
Step 2: "Now create a presentation outline based on these trends"
Step 3: "Write speaker notes for slide 2"
```

**Ask:** "How does chaining improve results?"

**Task:** Try chaining with your data

**Chain:**
1. **Step 1:** "Analyze this data and identify the top 3 trends"
2. **Step 2:** "Based on these trends, create 3 strategic recommendations"
3. **Step 3:** "Write an email summary for the CEO about these findings"

**Use the same dataset from Module 4, or choose your own data**

### **Quick Tips (2 minutes)**
- System prompts for consistency
- Few-shot learning with examples
- Optimization through iteration
